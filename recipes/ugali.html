Ugali

Description

Kenyan ugali is a staple food made from maize (corn) flour and water. It is a dense, firm, and slightly crumbly dish that resembles a thick porridge or polenta. To prepare ugali, maize flour is gradually stirred into boiling water until it thickens to a dough-like consistency.

It is then cooked while continuously stirring until it pulls away from the sides of the pot and forms a solid mass. Ugali is traditionally served alongside vegetables, meats, or stews, and is often eaten by hand, using small pieces to scoop up the accompanying dishes. It is a central part of Kenyan cuisine and is enjoyed across the country.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups maize (corn) flour
  • 4 cups water
  • Optional: A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Boil Water
  2. Add Maize Flour
  3. Cook and Stir
  4. Achieve the Right Consistency
  5. Finish Cooking
  6. Serve
