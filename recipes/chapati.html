Chapati

>

Description

Kenyan chapati is a popular flatbread that is soft, layered, and slightly chewy, with a golden-brown, crispy exterior. Made from wheat flour, water, salt, and sometimes a little oil or ghee, the dough is rolled out, folded, and then rolled again to create thin layers.

It is traditionally cooked on a hot griddle until both sides are well browned. Chapati is a staple in many Kenyan households and is often served with stews, vegetables, or meat dishes, making it a versatile and beloved component of Kenyan cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Wheat flour - The main ingredient,
  • usually all-purpose flour.
  • Water - Used to form the dough.
  • Salt - For seasoning the dough.
  • Oil or ghee - Used in the dough and also for
  • cooking to give the chapati a soft and slightly crispy texture.

Steps

  1. 3 cups all-purpose wheat flour
  2. 1 cup warm water (or a mix of water and milk)
  3. 1 teaspoon salt
