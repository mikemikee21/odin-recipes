Mandazi

Description

Kenyan mandazi is a popular East African snack that resembles a doughnut but is less sweet and denser. It is a type of fried bread made from a dough of flour, sugar, and other ingredients, which is cut into triangles or other shapes and deep-fried until golden brown.

Mandazi can be enjoyed on its own, with tea, or as a complement to other dishes. It's a common breakfast or snack item in Kenyan households and is known for its slightly sweet, subtly spiced flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cardamom (optional)
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk or regular milk
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons butter or margarine (softened)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients
  2. Incorporate Butter
  3. Add Wet Ingredients
  4. Knead the Dough
  5. Rest the Dough
  6. Roll and Cut
  7. Heat Oil
  8. Fry the Mandazi
  9. Drain and Serve
